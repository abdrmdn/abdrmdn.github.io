A Visit to Syria

I’m a Software engineer who was born and raised in Damascus, Syria.

I’ve loved this city but due to my curiosity and the country’s situation I had to leave the country 4 years ago and I’m working abroad since then.

I’ve decided to visit Damascus couple of months ago, and I remembered that a lot of people ask me how is it there, so I thought to myself why not document the visit so people can be more aware of how it is it then.

So! I wrote this story.

Who’s this post for ?

This story is mostly made for people who are not from Syria, or unaware of the situation happening in the capital of Syria Damascus.

As you can tell, those photos are old and were taken before the events started in Syria, but dont worry its not that bad now.

Now i want you to be very clear that im talking only about Damascus here, dont get the wrong impression that other cities are not burned to the ground.

Damascus is protected well by the government during the events, even though it had stuff happening but people there are still considered “blessed” for being in the safest of all cities somehow.

photos next can give you a very good idea about how bad it got in other cities.

YUP! that bad.

How does the city look from inside?

The city looks pretty normal from inside, beside the fact that it might need some work regarding being clean, especially in winter.

Its still clean and all.but, its just not as it was before.

Work, RelationShips, and life in current events?

As you can see in picture, people are living a normal life

Eating, working, shops are opened.

Nothing stopped, people adjusted! got used to whats happening around them, learned how to avoid troubles, learned how stay in a functional society without being affected by the war that is happening around them.

WORK

At the begaining people halted from going to their businesses, or continuing whatever brings income at the beginning of the war. but, after a while, the need for income rose. They wanted to provide for the loved ones. So, they adapted, going work whenever they feel its mostly safe, then more frequent, and more, and more. until they reached a point where they started thinking the following:

If something is going to happen to me, its going to be here or there!

and life goes on.

RELATIONSHIPS

Different types of relation-ships building in Syria. You’ve got the traditional which mostly happen by the act of liking between 2 and then introducing families to each other and making the relation-ship more offical wether dating/engagement or some other form of commitments which honestly isnt bad as some might perceive it. but, mainly its something agreed upon by both parties which makes it easy on both sides. The other type is normal dating, where 2 people meet, keep dating for a while and then they might move the relationship to another level.

But what I’m mainly saying here is that people are experiencing this now and having happy moments and sad moments like any other human being on this earth.

WHO’S STILL IN DAMASCUS? AND WHICH AGE GROUPS OF PEOPLE ARE LEFT?

Damascus became the place safest for those in other cities in Syria.

People come from all around seeking normal~ish life and safety, ofcourse with that comes different cultures, different tongues, and different morals. which makes Damascus a mix of varied culture currently.
And dont wonder if yes! people from different cities vary a lot in culture in our country.

WHICH AGE GROUP OF PEOPLE ARE LEFT THERE?

Well, thats easy.

NOT YOUTH PEOPLE (18–35)

Most of them are either refugees, working/studying abroad, prisoners, or dead(may they RIP).

Which causes the scene that we would see now in the street, a lot of girls involved in all kinds of businesses. which to be honest was something not common in Syria, so we moved from none to all sadly. Of course dont get me wrong, the community is educated enough to believe in equality, and there’s no sexism. Its just that it was something we had from the previous generation.

WHATS NEXT?

People are split in those categories now:

  • Refugees who are waiting for things to end to go back home.
  • Refugees who left and trying to get another nationality cause they’ve had it up their heads being syrian and all.
  • Those who afforded and were able to travel for studies whether paid/discounted/or else are probably settling outside for a while.
  • Those who afforded and were able to travel for work, and either not looking back, or those who are providing for their families that are left in Syria.
  • Those who afforded and were able to travel for work and wishing to get a citizen ship from another country.
  • Some that are allies and really believe in the government and want to stick in Syria believing they would wrap the issue soon (every year people hope it’ll be the next).
  • Some that feel so attached to people around them, their homes, what they built, and decided to stick around.
  • Some that stayed believing they can help who’s in need in their.
  • … and a lot others. but, those are the main ones.

And believe me none of those are mistaken for their choices, they chose what worked for them and for their families.

Now, what do you think is next?

Well, whats NEXT is that people are hoping things to go back the way it was, and all this mess to be wrapped up. of course, one wishes so. but, we’ve been saying that for years now. people currently are trying to build the country or at least starting from Damascus.

WHAT’S NEXT IS UNKNOWN! TOO MANY HANDS! TOO MUCH GREED! TOO MUCH VIOLENCE.

But, what can happen next, is that we’re welcome outside our country. able to live like any other human being. not being prevented from opening bank account, or told that you’re blackListed, or being looked at in sympathy. we’re human you know! we feel! we dont like to be treated either in a tough way nor in a sympathy. we have great skills and we can prove it to the world. and believe me we’re more motivated than most of the rest.

SUMMARY

Yes! I said we’re living normally and all. but, do note that some stuff still happen every once in a while. people are getting shot at, imprisoned or shattered in between Israel/ISIS/etc,.. attacks.

We want people to acknowledge us as normal human beings.

We want to feel like we belong somewhere!

We always have the fear of what if i had to leave tomorrow! where would i go..!

And last but not least, I leave you with some photos of Damascus.
